Tutorial - Uploading Records
   
 
 This tutorial is assuming you already connected to your website database,
 have searched for  your database for records, and they are displayed on your spread-sheet.
 
 The "Replace into tablename" MySQL command is used for uploading records into database.
 It is assumed that one of fields in you database table is a unique index field (usually auto-incremented).
 
  There are multiple ways to upload your database records from the spread-sheet.
  You can upload all records on the spread-sheet by clicking the Upload Record(s) button on the spread-sheet (top right hand corner).
It will upload all records on spread-sheet to your website database.
  You can also upload an individual record by selecting from the top spread-sheet menu, [Data] -> [Upload Selected Record to Database].
It will upload the selected row record to your database.
One other way is right clicking over the selected row in your spread sheet and clicking [Upload Selected Record to Database].
Or, if your using the Record Edit box to edit the record, instead of editing the record directly on the spread-sheet,  it also comes with a Upload Record button and menu.
   
  If you are editing various records, one by one, you can add them to the Pending Queries list on your spread-sheet and then upload them all at once.
